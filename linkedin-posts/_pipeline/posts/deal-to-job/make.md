So how does Make handle this behind the scenes?

It all starts when your CRM sends a webhook the moment someone changes a deal status to "won." Make receives that event and immediately starts parsing the deal information — customer details, project scope, contract value, all of it.

The system then checks if there are any addendums or change orders attached to this deal. This is where most manual processes break down, but Make just loops through each change order automatically, making sure nothing gets missed.

Here's the clever part: before creating duplicate job records, the automation checks if this deal already has a job assigned. You'd be surprised how often sales teams mark deals as "won" multiple times, especially when they're celebrating big wins.

Once it confirms this is genuinely a new job, Make processes all the data and starts populating your downstream systems. Customer information flows into your job management platform. Project details get added to your production schedule. Your crew gets notified about the new work coming their way.

The whole thing happens in under 30 seconds, and your team doesn't even know it's running until they see the perfectly organized job records waiting for them.
